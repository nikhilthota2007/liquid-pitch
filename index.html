How Deposits Work

Students deposit once. Their discount locks in.

When a student deposits money into Liquid, the size of that deposit determines the discount rate they receive. Liquid sets the deposit tier brackets by category of business — for restaurants, the brackets might be $0–$50 and $50–$100. Within those brackets, you set your own discount percentages.

You might offer 10% off at the first tier and 20% at the second, while the restaurant next door might choose 5% and 15%. That is entirely your decision — you choose the percentages that work for your margins and your goals. Liquid simply defines the bracket structure so the system stays consistent for students across similar types of businesses.

Each deposit is independent. If a student deposits $100 on Monday and $30 on Thursday, those are two separate balances with two separate discount rates. When they pay, the app spends from the oldest deposit first. Once that balance runs out, the next deposit's rate takes over.

All deposits are processed through Stripe with a 2.9% processing fee that goes entirely to Stripe — Liquid does not profit from deposits in any way.

Fund Lifecycle

Funds follow the academic year. Clean and simple.

All funds in Liquid follow an academic-year cycle. Regardless of when a student deposits — whether September or April — all balances expire at the end of June. When the new semester begins, the slate resets and students deposit fresh.

This keeps the system aligned with how students actually budget: semester by semester, year by year. No complex expiry calculations, no rolling windows — just one clear cycle that everyone understands.

Why this matters for you

Students are motivated to use their balances before the year ends. No dormant dollars sitting idle — every dollar deposited is actively looking for a meal at your restaurant. And every September, the cycle resets with a fresh wave of deposits from returning and incoming students.

Your Discount Tiers

You set the discounts. You're in control.

Liquid defines the tier brackets by business category so the system stays consistent for students. For restaurants, the brackets are based on deposit size. Within those brackets, every merchant sets their own discount percentages independently.

You choose the numbers that make sense for your business. A higher discount attracts more students and drives more volume. A lower discount protects your margins. The right balance depends on your goals — and you can adjust any time.

The only rule: higher deposit tiers must receive equal or better discounts than lower tiers. A student who commits more money should never get a worse deal.

Think of the discount as your customer acquisition cost. You're not losing margin — you're investing a small percentage per meal to lock in a customer who spends 3–4x more at your shop every month.

Off-Peak Hour Discounts

Turn your dead hours into revenue.

You know which hours are slow. With Liquid, you can drive foot traffic to those exact hours — without running your own marketing or spending a dollar on ads.

Here's how it works: You tell Liquid which hours are off-peak and the single discount percentage you want to offer. That's it. Liquid handles the rest.

When the off-peak window activates:

  • Students whose existing discount is already higher than your off-peak offer keep their current rate — their loyalty is never downgraded.
  • Students whose normal discount is lower get bumped up to your off-peak percentage for the duration of that window.

Your promotion only reaches students who would benefit — pulling in people who might not otherwise have chosen your restaurant during those hours, without over-discounting your most loyal customers.

Automatic push notifications. The moment your off-peak window opens, Liquid sends a push notification to every student in the area — your name, the discount, and the hours. No manual work from you.

Liquid's fee: 8% take rate

On every transaction during an off-peak window, Liquid charges 8% on the transaction value. That covers the platform, payment infrastructure, notifications, and the student network. No monthly fees, no subscriptions. At 8%, it's less than half what DoorDash charges.
